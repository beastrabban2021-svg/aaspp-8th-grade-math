Section 1: Test Overview & Structure

The California Assessment of Student Performance and Progress (CAASPP) is a system of assessments designed to measure student mastery of California learning standards. The 8th grade mathematics test is part of the Smarter Balanced Summative Assessments (SBSA).

Key Features

  • Computer-adaptive test: Questions adjust based on student responses (harder if correct, easier if incorrect)
  • Two parts: Computer Adaptive Test (CAT) and Performance Task (PT)
  • Technology-enhanced questions: Includes drag-and-drop, graph drawing, and data analysis
  • Calculator use: Scientific calculator allowed for grade 8 (available at calculator.smarterbalanced.org)
  • Reference sheet: Provided during the test with formulas and conversions

Test Structure

Component Description
Claim 1 Concepts and Procedures (Priority Cluster)
Claims 2 & 4 Problem Solving / Modeling and Data Analysis (combined score)
Claim 3 Communicating Reasoning
Note: The test is untimed, but most students complete it within 2-3 hours for the CAT portion and 45-60 minutes for the Performance Task.

Section 2: Content Domains & Standards

Domain Key Standards Description
The Number System 8.NS.1, 8.NS.2 Know that there are numbers that are not rational, and approximate them by rational numbers; work with radicals and integer exponents
Expressions & Equations 8.EE.1-8 Work with radicals and integer exponents; understand connections between proportional relationships, lines, and linear equations; analyze and solve linear equations and pairs of simultaneous linear equations
Functions 8.F.1-5 Define, evaluate, and compare functions; use functions to model relationships between quantities
Geometry 8.G.1-9 Understand congruence and similarity using physical models; understand and apply the Pythagorean Theorem; solve problems involving volume of cylinders, cones, and spheres
Statistics & Probability 8.SP.1-4 Investigate patterns of association in bivariate data; construct and interpret scatter plots; use linear models

Section 6: Performance Task Example

Volume and Surface Area Performance Task

Scenario: A company manufactures three sizes of cylindrical containers for storing food. The containers have the following dimensions:

  • Small: radius = 3 inches, height = 8 inches
  • Medium: radius = 4 inches, height = 10 inches
  • Large: radius = 6 inches, height = 12 inches
Item 1
What is the volume of each container? Round your answer to the nearest cubic inch. Show your work or explain your answer.
Small: 226 in³
Medium: 502 in³
Large: 1357 in³
Item 2
The company wants to create a label that wraps around the lateral surface of each container. What is the area of the label needed for each container? Round your answer to the nearest square inch. Show your work or explain your answer.
Small: 151 in²
Medium: 251 in²
Large: 452 in²
Item 3
The company is considering creating a new container that has the same height as the large container (12 inches) but with a volume of approximately 1,000 cubic inches. What should the radius of the new container be? Round your answer to the nearest tenth of an inch. Show your work or explain your answer.
Answer: r ≈ 5.1 inches

Section 7: Reference Sheet & Formulas

Conversions

Length:

  • 1 inch = 2.54 centimeters
  • 1 meter = 39.37 inches
  • 1 mile = 5,280 feet
  • 1 mile = 1,760 yards
  • 1 mile = 1.609 kilometers
  • 1 kilometer = 0.62 mile

Weight:

  • 1 pound = 16 ounces
  • 1 pound = 0.454 kilogram
  • 1 kilogram = 2.2 pounds
  • 1 ton = 2,000 pounds

Capacity:

  • 1 cup = 8 fluid ounces
  • 1 pint = 2 cups
  • 1 quart = 2 pints
  • 1 gallon = 4 quarts
  • 1 gallon = 3.785 liters
  • 1 liter = 0.264 gallon
  • 1 liter = 1,000 cubic centimeters

Formulas

Triangle Area A = ½bh
Parallelogram Area A = bh
Circle Area A = πr²
Circle Circumference C = πd or C = 2πr
General Prisms Volume V = Bh
Cylinder Volume V = πr²h
Sphere Volume V = (4/3)πr³
Cone Volume V = (1/3)πr²h
Pythagorean Theorem a² + b² = c²

Tips for Success

  1. Read carefully: Understand what the question is asking before solving
  2. Show your work: For constructed response questions, partial credit is available
  3. Use tools wisely: Calculator, ruler, protractor, and reference sheet are available
  4. Check units: Make sure your answer matches the units requested
  5. Manage time: Don't spend too long on any single question
  6. Technology practice: Familiarize yourself with drag-and-drop, graphing, and equation editors
